HOW DO COLORS IMPACT?

Can I paint this color for my child's room? Will it be too bright? Is there enough stimulation for the baby? These are some of the most common questions about how colors affect your baby. While there is still much scientific debate about how colors affect babies, there is a wealth of evidence to show how colors affect adults, it is no coincidence that most food restaurants Both colors are red and yellow, which research shows can make you feel hungrier.

Why not take advantage of all this research and use it to choose colors for your child's bedroom? While your baby may not perceive colors correctly for a while, as they get older they will. Here's a quick rundown of some color theories and how they can help you choose the right paint color for your child's room.

Red - Passion, excitement, emotion

The bold red color attracts a lot of attention. It can be used in the bedroom as an accent, but too much red should be avoided. Red is the “hottest” color and can evoke whimsical personality traits to stand out.

Orange - Warm, comfortable, cozy

Orange is a very pleasant color. It promotes a sense of welcome and inspires interpersonal conversation. Cam is user-friendly and comfortable to use. Use a darker orange for a super cozy space or a bright orange for a modern touch!

Yellow - Vibrant, energetic, cheerful

Yellow represents sunshine and light, but use it with caution. Too much or too bright yellow can make the baby agitated. Subtle yellow promotes concentration and emotional thinking.

Green - Soothes, refreshes, nourishes

Green is the best color to use in a learning environment. It promotes calmness of thought and concentration. It is also very serene, calming and natural. Green is one of the best colors in decoration because it is abundant in nature.

Purple - Sovereign, mysterious, luxurious

Purple has long been associated with royalty and that remains to this day. Jewel tone purple is as regal as it gets. Pastel purples, like lavenders and lilacs, are softer and more elegant but still feel luxurious. Purple is very rare in nature therefore it is the most “unnatural” color.

White - Pure and safe

White is angelic and sweet but can also promote secrecy. Stay away from all white, use splashes of color to evoke emotion and openness. Be careful white is definitely easy to stain!
